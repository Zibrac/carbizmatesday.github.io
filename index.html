What is Mate's Day

Connection
saves lives.

MAYEIGHT is Movember's official day of mateship, held each year on May 8. It's a day to reconnect with someone you haven't caught up with in a while — a mate, a colleague, a sibling, a neighbour.

But loneliness doesn't discriminate. One in four Australians experience it at some point, and 25% of young Australians aged 15–24 report persistent loneliness. Whether you identify as male, female, or non-binary — reaching out matters. For everyone.

Three simple steps

How to show up
this Mate's Day

01
📱
Tee it up

Don't be the one waiting. They're probably waiting too. Be the first to send that text, DM, or voice note. It takes ten seconds and it might mean everything.

02
📅
Catch up

Lock it in. Saying "let's catch up soon" without a time and place goes nowhere. Set a day, a time, a location — coffee, a walk, a video call. Make it real.

03
💬
Speak up

Ask how they're really going — and actually listen. You don't need answers, just be present. The conversation matters more than you know.
